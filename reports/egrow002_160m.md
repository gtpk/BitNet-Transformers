## Verdict: localization is NOT a lever -- top-k does not beat random-k or none

A sidecar on the B_l-top-k layers (down_proj-heavy, the high-sensitivity sites) did NOT beat a
type-matched, byte-fair random-k sidecar -- it was nominally the WORST arm (0.148 < randk 0.185 <
none 0.222). So the stable, sensitivity-based EGROW-001 ranking does not translate into useful
targeted capacity: putting the auxiliary organ on the "right" layers buys nothing over random
placement, and any small sidecar (300-step co-adapt, k=5) slightly under-performs no sidecar here.

Caveat: 27-item panel, 1 seed -- 0.148/0.185/0.222 is 4/5/6 hits, within ~+-2-hit seed noise (the
none arm is 0.222 vs SIDE-001's rank0 0.185, showing run-to-run variance). So the strong "top-k is
worst" reading is noisy; the robust conclusion is the ABSENCE of a positive localization signal --
if locating growth by B_l helped, top-k would clearly exceed random-k, and it does not.
